Australian Interest Rates in 2026: What Home Buyers Need to Know

The RBA cash rate has stabilised after a volatile period. Here's what it means for your home loan and whether now is the right time to lock in.

Home/Blog/Interest Rates 2026
June 15, 2026 Interest Rates

The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) has held the cash rate at 4.35% since late 2025, bringing an end to a period of significant volatility that saw rates move from historic lows to their highest level in over two decades.

Where Are We Now?

After 13 consecutive rate rises between May 2022 and November 2023, the RBA began cutting rates in early 2025. The current stance reflects a balancing act — keeping inflation near the target band (2-3%) while supporting economic growth.

The RBA has signalled that further rate moves are data-dependent, with housing affordability and household spending being key factors to watch.

What This Means for Home Loan Borrowers

Variable Rate Borrowers

If you're on a variable rate, your repayments may have adjusted as lenders respond to RBA decisions. Most major banks have passed through recent cuts, but the full effect can take time.

Floating Rate Borrowers

Fixed-rate deals are becoming more available again as lenders gain confidence in rate stability. If you've been waiting for a better fixed deal, now may be worth exploring — though rates are still higher than the historic lows of 2021-2022.

New Borrowers

For first home buyers entering the market now, interest rates are stabilising. This makes it easier to forecast your repayments and plan your budget with more certainty than in recent years.

Should You Lock In a Fixed Rate?

The decision to fix depends on your risk tolerance and financial situation:

  • Fix if you want payment certainty, are close to your budget limit, or believe rates may rise again.
  • Stay variable if you have offset accounts, redraw facilities, or think rates may fall further.
  • Consider splitting — fix part of your loan for certainty and keep the rest variable for flexibility.

The Bottom Line

Interest rate uncertainty is easing, but rates are unlikely to return to the sub-2% levels seen in 2020-2021. Smart borrowers are focusing on what they can control — choosing the right loan features, comparing lenders regularly, and working with a broker who has access to the full market.
