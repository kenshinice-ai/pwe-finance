First Home Buyer Grants & Incentives: Victoria Update 2026

New changes to the First Home Owner Grant and stamp duty concessions in Victoria. Here's what first home buyers need to know this year.

Home/Blog/FHOG Victoria 2026
May 20, 2026 First Home Buyers

If you're a first home buyer in Victoria, there are several government grants and concessions available that can significantly reduce the cost of purchasing your first property. Here's a comprehensive guide to what's on offer in 2026.

First Home Owner Grant (FHOG)

The Victorian First Home Owner Grant provides eligible first home buyers with a $10,000 grant towards the purchase or construction of a new home.

Eligibility Criteria:

  • You must be at least 18 years old
  • You (and your spouse/partner) must not have owned property in Australia before
  • The property must be a new home (unregistered house and land contract or newly built home)
  • The property value must not exceed $600,000
  • You must move into the property within 12 months of settlement and live there for at least 6 continuous months

Stamp Duty Concessions & Exemptions

VicRevenue offers stamp duty relief for first home buyers based on property value:

Full Exemption: Properties up to $300,000

If you're purchasing a new home valued at $300,000 or less, you'll pay no stamp duty at all. This can save you thousands.

Sliding Scale: Properties between $300,001 and $600,000

If your property falls in this range, you'll receive a sliding scale concession that reduces your stamp duty progressively. For example, a $500,000 new home saves approximately $14,000 in stamp duty.

Note: Stamp duty concessions for existing homes (resale) are available up to $600,000 with a sliding scale concession. Full exemption applies up to $295,000.

First Home Guarantee Scheme

The Commonwealth Government's First Home Guarantee scheme allows eligible first home buyers to purchase with just a 5% deposit without paying Lender's Mortgage Insurance (LMI). The government guarantees up to 15% of the property value on your behalf.

Key Benefits:

  • Only 5% deposit required (instead of 20%)
  • No LMI — saving thousands in upfront costs
  • Available for both new homes and existing properties
  • Income caps apply: $125,000 for singles, $200,000 for couples (2026)

Other State-Based Incentives

VicRevenue also offers the Home Start Grant ($10,000) for eligible first home buyers purchasing or building a new home. Combined with FHOG and stamp duty concessions, you could receive up to $20,000 in grants plus significant stamp duty savings.

Tips for First Home Buyers

  • Get pre-approved first — know your budget before you start looking
  • Consider new builds — they qualify for FHOG and stamp duty exemptions
  • Look at growth corridors — outer Melbourne suburbs offer better affordability with good infrastructure investment
  • Work with a broker — we can identify all grants you're eligible for and structure your loan optimally
